<Info>
  If you already have a saved pin sequence, click **Click Here** at the top right of the generator to load it and skip regeneration entirely.
</Info>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the generator">
    Navigate to [https://halfmonty.github.io/StringArtGenerator/](https://halfmonty.github.io/StringArtGenerator/) and wait until the status reads **"Generator is ready."** before proceeding.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose an image">
    Click **"Click Here and select an Image to Start"** and choose a photo from your device.

    <Tip>
      Close-up, high-contrast photos (portraits, silhouettes) produce the most recognizable string art.
    </Tip>

    The image is automatically center-cropped to a square, resized to 500×500 pixels, converted to grayscale, and masked to a circle before processing begins.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for generation">
    The generator runs three phases in sequence. Watch the status indicator for progress:

    1. **"Calculating pins…"** — places 288 pins evenly around the hoop
    2. **"Precalculating all lines…"** — computes pixel coordinates for every possible pin-pair line
    3. **"Drawing Lines…"** — greedy selection loop runs up to 4,000 iterations, darkening the canvas one string at a time

    <Note>
      The page may become unresponsive or crash on very large images. Refreshing the page restores the tool to its ready state.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the output">
    Once complete, the string art canvas appears on screen. The **drawStatus** field shows the total number of lines drawn and the percentage complete.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save your pin sequence">
    Copy the numbers from the textarea at the bottom of the page. This pin sequence can be pasted back into the generator later to redisplay your string art without regenerating it from scratch.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Build your piece">
    - Click **"Start Creating"** to open the step-by-step assistant, which guides you through wrapping each string one pin at a time.
    - Click **"Just Draw"** to auto-render the full sequence immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>
